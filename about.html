I was born in Winchester, which is only ten miles north of where I'm currently studying, I decided on Southampton after looking around at Birmingham, Bristol, Reading and Warwick. I'd been impressed by Southampton after doing the Student Robotics Challenge through my sixth form college, Peter Symonds, Winchester.

I've been surrounded by technology from a very early age, and I'm interested in hardware as well as software. I've built electronic projects for fun as a child, and more recently built my own PCs. I'm particularly interested in the open source movement, and I'm a keen Linux (Ubuntu) user. I like to help other people use this sort of software and quite often advise users over Internet Relay Chat.

In my summer holidays for the last few years I've worked for the IBM Storage Subsystems group in Hursley, this group develop virtual storage systems, and high end RAID systems.

As a hobby, I'm keen on ice skating, which I took up after a chance outing with the Scouts. I became hooked on skating and went early ever Saturday morning for several years, in fact now I'm here at Southampton, I'm missing skating quite a bit. Hopefully I'll be able to find time over the Christmas break to start again.

I'm also very interested in science, I've had a subscription to New Scientist for four or five years, and keep up to date with all the latest developments in the scientific world.
